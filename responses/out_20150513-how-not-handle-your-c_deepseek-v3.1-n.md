# How Not to Handle Your Clinical Data (May 2015)

## 1. SUMMARY
This commentary criticizes Orexigen Therapeutics for prematurely disclosing interim cardiovascular benefit data from the first 25% of their ongoing obesity drug trial for Contrave (a sustained-release combination of bupropion and naltrexone). The FDA stated they would not accept the compromised trial results, leading to the study's termination and Orexigen's breach-of-contract issues with partner Takeda. The article argues that Orexigen violated fundamental principles of clinical trial conduct by releasing interim data that ultimately proved unreliable as the cardiovascular benefit became statistically insignificant in later analyses.
