# Metal Impurities Will Waste Your Time (January 2013)

## 1. SUMMARY

This 2013 article highlights a critical quality control issue in drug discovery: metal impurities in screening compounds causing false positive results. The Roche group described how zinc residues from synthesis (up to 20% by mass) made compounds appear active in assays, but the activity disappeared when the compounds were resynthesized using different routes without metal-based reduction steps. The article warned that many drug discovery programs had likely been misled by such metal contamination, particularly from zinc and copper, which can coordinate with compounds and create assay artifacts. The authors recommended using zinc chelators like TPEN as counterscreens and being especially suspicious of compounds made via Ullmann reactions (copper-mediated couplings) or those showing unusual colors like green.
