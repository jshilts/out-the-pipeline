**Gene-Environment Interactions:** The field increasingly recognized that genetic risk factors interact with environmental triggers (stress, trauma, substance use, sleep disruption). The diathesis-stress model became more prominent, with genetics seen as creating susceptibility rather than determining outcomes.

**Clinical Genetics:** Despite genetic advances, clinical diagnosis of bipolar disorder remains based on psychiatric evaluation rather than genetic testing. No genetic panels have achieved clinical utility for diagnosis, treatment selection, or risk prediction in routine practice.
