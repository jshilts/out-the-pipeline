# Analysis of "Big Journals, Big Retractions" (2014)

## 1. SUMMARY

The article addresses the observation that high-impact journals tend to have higher retraction rates. The author identifies two primary mechanisms driving this phenomenon: first, that "splashy, cutting-edge" research with inherently higher error rates naturally gravitates toward prestigious journals, and second, that high-profile publications receive greater scrutiny from the scientific community, making errors more likely to be detected and corrected through retraction. The author expresses little surprise at this correlation, suggesting it aligns with reasonable expectations about how scientific publication and error correction function in practice.

Studies suggest both mechanisms operate, with evidence that high-impact journals see both more honest errors being corrected AND unfortunately, more cases of misconduct.

**Retraction Growth:** The period following 2014 saw a dramatic increase in retractions across all journals, partly due to improved detection methods (including AI-based software), greater transparency, and systematic fraud detection efforts. By 2022-2023, retraction rates had increased several-fold from 2014 levels.

**Disciplinary Patterns:** Research revealed that retraction patterns vary significantly by field. While biomedical research dominated retraction statistics (partly due to volume and impact), other fields showed different patterns of error correction.
