# Bias in Industry-Funded Trials in Rheumatoid Arthritis? (February 2012)

## 1. SUMMARY

This 2012 article reports on a meta-analysis examining whether industry funding influenced outcomes of randomized controlled trials for rheumatoid arthritis drugs. The study analyzed 103 recent clinical trials, with over half being industry-funded. The counterintuitive finding was that funding source did not predict study outcomes—trials from industry, nonprofit, and unspecified funding sources all tended to favor the tested drugs at similar rates.

The industrial trials did show methodological differences: they enrolled more subjects, provided better documentation of intent-to-treat protocols, and focused more on newer drugs compared to nonprofit trials that studied older drug combinations. However, after controlling for factors like drug type, number of centers, study phase, and journal impact factor, no association remained between funding source and trial outcomes. The one concerning finding was evidence of publication bias—industry-sponsored studies appeared less frequently in the literature, though it was unclear if this represented suppressed unfavorable results.
