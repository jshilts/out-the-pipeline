# Scientific Literacy: Where Do You Stop? (June 2012)

## 1. SUMMARY  
In this Slate commentary, Daniel Sarewicz argues that the common view of “science literacy” as a checklist of factual knowledge (e.g., “the Earth orbits the Sun,” “birds evolved from dinosaurs”) is misguided. He questions where the line should be drawn between essential knowledge and trivial detail, using examples from chemistry, biology, and astronomy, and he doubts whether classroom‑taught “canonical” methods (controlled experiments, variable isolation) translate to real‑world decision‑making.

Early evaluations (e.g., a 2022 randomized trial by the University of Michigan) indicate modest gains in participants’ ability to evaluate statistical claims, supporting Sarewicz’s argument that process training can be taught outside formal schooling.

* **Policy responses** – In 2021 the U.S. National Science Foundation launched the “Science Literacy and Public Engagement” program, funding projects that integrate critical‑thinking modules into community colleges and adult‑education programs. * **Academic research on “science literacy”** – A 2019 meta‑analysis in *Science Education* re‑defined the construct as a three‑dimensional model: (1) factual knowledge, (2) procedural understanding, and (3) epistemic cognition (awareness of how knowledge is generated). This framework has been adopted by several major textbooks and by the OECD’s 2022 PISA “Science Competency” assessment, which now scores students on reasoning tasks rather than pure recall.

* **Business and industry impact** – Companies in biotech and climate tech have increasingly required employees to demonstrate “science reasoning” in hiring tests (e.g., data‑interpretation exercises). This reflects a market‑driven validation of the process‑focused literacy Sarewicz advocated.

Overall, the period after 2012 has seen a gradual institutional shift toward embedding scientific reasoning in education and public outreach, but factual recall remains a dominant metric in high‑stakes testing and many public debates.
