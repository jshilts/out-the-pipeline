# ChemDraw Days (August 2014)

## 1. SUMMARY
This article is a personal retrospective by Derek Lowe about the early days of ChemDraw, a pioneering software program for drawing chemical structures. The author reflects on his own graduate school experience transitioning from manual structure drawing methods—using green plastic templates for cyclohexane conformations, rub-on letter transfers, and physically cutting and pasting schemes together with tape—to the revolutionary experience of using ChemDraw on early Macintosh computers. The article captures both the technical limitations of the era (disk failures, multiple backups in paranoia) and the cultural transition as senior advisors unfamiliar with computing marveled at basic features like "Find and Replace."
