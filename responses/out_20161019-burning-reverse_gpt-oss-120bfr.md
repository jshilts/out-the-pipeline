The dominant cost driver remains the high cell voltage and the need for gas‑phase CO₂ purification.  

* **Policy and funding** – The US Department of Energy’s **Electrochemical CO₂ Reduction (ECR) Program** and the EU’s **Carbon Capture and Utilisation (CCU)** initiatives have funded >$300 M in research, but the focus has shifted toward **CO** and **formate** as platform intermediates rather than direct ethanol production.  

* **Commercial reality** – As of late 2025, **no commercial facility** produces ethanol from CO₂ electrochemically, and no major biotech or petrochemical firm has announced a rollout of such technology. The field remains at the **TRL 4‑5** (lab‑scale demonstration) stage.

**Business outcomes**  
* Oak Ridge National Laboratory continues to publish on nanostructured Cu catalysts, but no spin‑out company based on the “nanospike” electrode has reached market.  
* Companies that pursued CO₂ electro‑reduction have pivoted to **syngas** or **formic acid** products, where the economics are more favorable.
