# Sunlight And the Brain (Oct 2018)

The authors report that UV light raises levels of urocanic acid (UCA) in the bloodstream; UCA can cross the blood‑brain barrier, be converted by urocanase into glutamate, and thereby increase neuronal glutamate concentrations. Using a novel “patch‑clamp mass‑spec” technique, they detected UCA in cerebrospinal fluid and in neurons across most brain regions (except the nucleus accumbens). The authors extrapolate that this UV‑activated UCA‑glutamate pathway could underlie sun‑related mood improvement, addiction to sun‑bathing, cognition, and brain development, offering a mechanistic explanation for seasonal affective phenomena.
