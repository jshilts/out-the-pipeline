### Norbornyl cation  
* **Consensus solidified:** High‑resolution gas‑phase infrared spectroscopy (e.g., 2015, 2020 studies) and advanced ab‑initio calculations (CCSD(T) with large basis sets) have confirmed the bridged, delocalized structure first inferred by Olah. The non‑classical ion is now a textbook example of hyperconjugative stabilization, and the debate is considered closed in mainstream organic chemistry curricula.

### Diels–Alder mechanism  
* **Experimental validation:** Time‑resolved X‑ray diffraction and kinetic isotope effect measurements (2014‑2022) have demonstrated that the reaction coordinate can be tuned from a synchronous to an asynchronous pathway by altering substituents or applying external electric fields. ### Molecular nanotechnology (MNT)  
* **Technological advances:** DNA origami, protein nanocages, and peptide‑based self‑assembly have achieved sub‑10 nm precision, enabling functional nanodevices (e.g., drug‑delivery cages, nanoscale reactors). * **Commercial outcomes:** No company has produced a commercial molecular assembler; the closest analogues are enzyme‑based biocatalytic processes and synthetic biology platforms that repurpose cellular machinery for chemical production (e.g., engineered *E. coli* producing artemisinin precursors at multi‑kiloton scale).

### Broader biotech landscape  
* **Enzyme therapeutics:** Since 2012, several enzyme replacement therapies (e.g., PEG‑asparaginase, recombinant human glucocerebrosidase) have received FDA approval, confirming that engineered enzymes can be safe and effective at therapeutic doses.
