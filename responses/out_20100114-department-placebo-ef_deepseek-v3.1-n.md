# Department of Placebo Effects (January 2010)

## 1. SUMMARY

The article describes a 2010 incident in a Johannesburg suburb where residents reported various health symptoms—rashes, headaches, nausea, and disrupted sleep—which they attributed to a newly installed cell phone tower. The residents believed they were being harmed by electromagnetic radiation from the tower. The key revelation reported in the article was that the tower had actually been switched off for six weeks prior to the hearing where residents detailed their complaints, making their reported symptoms difficult to explain by actual electromagnetic exposure. The article frames this as a nocebo effect (negative placebo), where the belief of being harmed produces real physical symptoms despite the absence of the supposed cause.
