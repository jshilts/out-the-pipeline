# Steven Weinberg's "Four Golden Lessons" for Scientists (January 2004)

## 1. SUMMARY

This article discusses Nobel laureate Steven Weinberg's advice to early-career scientists, originally published in Nature (November 27, 2003, p. 389). The "Four Golden Lessons" are:

1. **No one knows everything** - accept that you can't master an entire field and instead focus on fundamentals that enable you to learn what you need when you need it
2. **Aim for rough water** - pursue contentious, unsettled areas where creative work is still possible rather than well-established fields
3. **Forgive yourself for wasting time** - accept that research involves uncertain paths, dead ends, and efforts that may not lead to solutions, especially when working on poorly understood problems
4. **Learn the history of science** - understand how science actually progresses (not through idealized philosophical models) and gain perspective on your work's place in human civilization
