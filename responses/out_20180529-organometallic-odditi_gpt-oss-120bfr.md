While the reaction works in many solvents, yields are dramatically higher in N‑methyl‑2‑pyrrolidone (NMP).  Earlier work had shown that mixing MeMgBr with FeCl₃ in THF generates a mixed magnesium/iron cluster (a penta‑iron octa‑methyl anion paired with a Mg(THF)₅⁺ cation), but this species gave only modest yields.  

The new study (Angew. Chem. Int. Ed. 2018, DOI 10.1002/anie.201802087) demonstrated that in NMP a completely different ionic pair forms: a **magnesium hexa‑NMP dication** ([Mg(NMP)₆]²⁺) and **two trimethyl‑iron anions** ([FeMe₃]⁻).  Spectroscopic evidence (magnetic circular dichroism, Mössbauer) and X‑ray crystallography showed that NMP coordinates only to magnesium, leaving the iron centers “naked” and exceptionally reactive.  The authors argued that this solvent‑driven speciation explains the superior coupling efficiency and adds a new structural motif to the organometallic toolbox.
