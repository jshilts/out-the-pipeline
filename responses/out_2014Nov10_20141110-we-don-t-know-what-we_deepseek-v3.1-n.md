These primers, used for identifying bacteria and archaea in environmental samples, can miss major lineages due to primer mismatches. Additionally, metagenomic analyses have traditionally focused on matching known genes and genomes, creating inherent bias against discovering completely novel life forms.

These organisms might persist in extreme environments like deep rock formations, protected from the evolutionary pressures that shaped surface life.
