# Article Title (Month Year)
Fewer Die - But Why?
September 2015

## 1. SUMMARY

The article discusses the surprising cardiovascular mortality benefit observed with empagliflozin (Jardiance), an SGLT-2 inhibitor used for type 2 diabetes treatment. In an NEJM-published trial of approximately 7,000 patients, empagliflozin treatment showed a significant reduction in all-cause mortality (5.7% vs 8.3% in controls), representing a potentially substantial real-world impact of about 25,000 fewer deaths per million patients. The author notes several puzzling aspects: no clear dose-response relationship, no significant differences in myocardial infarction or stroke rates, but notable reductions in cardiovascular death, heart failure hospitalizations, and renal failure. Most intriguingly, the benefit did not correlate well with blood glucose control, suggesting unknown mechanisms beyond glycemic management. The author speculates this could be related to the drug's SGLT-2 inhibition mechanism, which causes glucose excretion in urine, but acknowledges this mechanism remains poorly understood and requires further investigation.

## 2. HISTORY

**Drug Approvals and Label Expansion:**
- Empagliflozin received FDA cardiovascular risk reduction indication in December 2016, becoming the first diabetes drug with proven cardiovascular mortality benefit
- Other SGLT-2 inhibitors subsequently demonstrated similar benefits through dedicated cardiovascular outcomes trials (CVOTs)
- Canagliflozin (CANVAS program, 2017) showed cardiovascular benefit but also increased fracture and amputation risks
- Dapagliflozin (DECLARE-TIMI 58, 2018) demonstrated heart failure hospitalization reduction

**Mechanistic Understanding Development:**
Research eventually elucidated that SGLT-2 inhibitors' benefits extend beyond glucose control through:
- Hemodynamic effects (blood pressure reduction, diuresis)
- Improved cardiac metabolism and bioenergetics
- Reduced inflammation and oxidative stress
- Anti-fibrotic effects
- Nephroprotective mechanisms

**Clinical Guidelines Evolution:**
- Major diabetes guidelines (ADA/EASD) now recommend SGLT-2 inhibitors as first-line therapy for patients with established cardiovascular disease or high cardiovascular risk
- Updated treatment algorithms prioritize SGLT-2 inhibitors alongside GLP-1 receptor agonists for patients with cardiovascular indications

**Broader Therapeutic Applications:**
- FDA approved dapagliflozin for heart failure with reduced ejection fraction (2020) based on landmark trials
- SGLT-2 inhibitors now approved for chronic kidney disease treatment (empagliflozin, dapagliflozin)
- This represents successful drug repurposing beyond diabetes

**Market Adoption:**
- SGLT-2 inhibitors achieved blockbuster status with billions in annual sales
- Prescribing patterns shifted from purely glycemic control to cardiovascular risk reduction
- Increased clinical uptake among cardiologists, not just endocrinologists

**Regulatory Impact:**
- The empagliflozin results accelerated FDA's 2008 mandate requiring cardiovascular safety trials for all new diabetes drugs
- Set new evidentiary standards for diabetes drug development
- Enabled cardiovascular outcome claims in drug labeling
