# More Supplement Than You Bargained For (August 2015)

## 1. SUMMARY

The article discusses serious contamination problems in the dietary supplement industry, highlighting cases where supplements contained undeclared active pharmaceutical ingredients like sildenafil (Viagra), phenolphthalein, or the synthetic stimulant DMBA. The author explains that the 1994 Dietary Supplement Health Education Act (DSHEA) created a regulatory framework that sharply curtailed FDA's oversight powers over supplements, treating them differently than pharmaceutical drugs.

Under DSHEA, supplement companies don't need FDA approval before marketing products, aren't required to demonstrate safety or efficacy, and can make certain structure/function claims as long as ingredients have historical use. The FDA can only act after a product is on the market and evidence shows it's unsafe. The author criticizes this as the "Snake Oil Protection Act" and argues it allows dangerous contamination to persist. The piece also discusses underlying consumer psychology around "natural vs. synthetic" beliefs and vitalism that drives supplement popularity while making regulation politically difficult.
