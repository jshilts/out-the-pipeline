# A Real Genetic Headscratcher (November 2007)

## 1. SUMMARY

The article discusses the puzzling discovery of "ultraconserved elements" - stretches of DNA hundreds of base pairs long that remain absolutely identical across mice, rats, and humans despite ~75 million years of evolutionary divergence. The natural assumption was that such perfect conservation meant these elements serve critical biological functions.

To test this hypothesis, researchers at Lawrence Berkeley and the DOE deleted four of these ultraconserved elements from mice. Surprisingly, the knockout mice appeared completely normal - born normally, grew up normally, and showed no detectable differences from wild-type mice in exhaustive biochemical and behavioral tests. This contradicted expectations that removing such perfectly conserved DNA would cause severe defects or lethality.

The mystery deepened because genes located near these ultraconserved sequences did prove to be essential when deleted. This created a fundamental paradox: how can DNA remain perfectly unchanged for 75 million years if it's apparently dispensable for survival and normal development?
