# Where Do All The Chemicals Go? (November 2006)

## 1. SUMMARY

This article describes the practical logistics and waste disposal challenges that arise when chemistry projects in a drug company are suddenly terminated. The author outlines a hierarchy of decision-making for handling different categories of chemicals: reaction mixtures in fume hoods typically go directly to waste disposal; opened solvent bottles are generally discarded due to contamination concerns and lack of traceability; unopened commercial reagents may be transferred to active sites, donated, or sold before becoming waste; and custom-synthesized compounds and intermediates receive preferential treatment for preservation and transfer. The most valuable category is the compound repository—the collection of vials containing substances that someone deemed worth making and testing—which represents such a significant investment in labor and cost that discarding it would be considered wasteful destruction of corporate assets, akin to "a coal company setting its mines on fire."
