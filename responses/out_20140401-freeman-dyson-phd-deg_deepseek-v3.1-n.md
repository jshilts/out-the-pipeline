# Freeman Dyson on the PhD Degree (April 2014)

## 1. SUMMARY
This article presents retired Caltech physicist and author John Brockman's interview with the National Humanities Medal-winning mathematician Freeman Dyson, in which Dyson expresses his strong opposition to the PhD degree. Dyson calls the PhD system "an abomination" and argues that it was designed for 19th century German professors but has since become an inappropriate "union card" required for academic and other jobs. He contends that the PhD forces people to waste years pretending to do research they're not suited for and that the resulting credential "doesn't really mean anything." Additionally, Dyson argues that the lengthy PhD process discourages women from becoming scientists, which he considers tragic. Despite his lifelong opposition, Dyson acknowledges he's had "no success at all" in changing the system.
