# Caloric Restriction and Lifespan - Without the Caloric Restriction? (December 2009)

## 1. SUMMARY

The article discusses a *Nature* study examining caloric restriction's effects on lifespan in fruit flies (Drosophila). The conventional understanding was that reduced caloric intake extends lifespan but at the cost of reduced fertility, creating a survival-versus-reproduction tradeoff mediated by overall metabolic energy availability.

The research took calorically restricted flies and systematically added back nutrient classes (vitamins, lipids, carbohydrates, proteins/amino acids) to identify which components drive longevity and fertility effects. Vitamins, lipids, and carbohydrates showed no significant impact on lifespan when reintroduced. However, amino acids—specifically essential amino acids—reproduced the full-feeding pattern: reduced lifespan coupled with restored reproduction. The crucial finding was that **methionine** alone could restore fecundity without shortening lifespan, while lifespan reduction required both methionine and other essential amino acids. This suggested that nutrient imbalance (particularly disproportionate methionine relative to other EAAs) rather than raw caloric intake drives these effects, potentially allowing dietary restriction benefits without sacrificing fertility.
