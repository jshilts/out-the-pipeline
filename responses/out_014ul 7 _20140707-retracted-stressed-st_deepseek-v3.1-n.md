## 2. HISTORY

The STAP cell scandal unfolded dramatically from January 2014 onward. What Lowe was commenting on in July 2014 was already the middle of the story:

**The Rise (January 2014):** Two Nature papers by Obokata et al. claimed that mature mouse cells could be converted to pluripotent stem cells simply by bathing them in weak acid or applying physical stress—a revolutionary technique that would have been far simpler than existing reprogramming methods like induced pluripotent stem cells (iPSCs).

**The Fall (February-June 2014):** Within weeks, other labs failed to replicate the results. Bloggers identified suspicious image duplications and manipulations. RIKEN launched an investigation in February, and by April 1, Obokata was found guilty of research misconduct. By June, all co-authors requested retraction. Nature officially retracted both papers on July 2, 2014, just days before Lowe's commentary.

**Aftermath (2014-2020):** The scientific consequences were severe. Senior co-author Yoshiki Sasai, deputy director of RIKEN, hanged himself in August 2014, leaving notes expressing shame over the scandal. Obokata's mentor Teruhiko Wakayama moved to the University of Yamanashi. Obokata herself was dismissed from RIKEN in December 2014. She published a popular book about the experience in 2015, maintaining her innocence regarding intentional fraud.

The institutional impact was significant: RIKEN overhauled its research integrity procedures, and major journals including Nature expanded their image screening protocols. The scientific community became more vigilant about image manipulation and replication requirements, partly spurred by this scandal and similar ones like the cardiac stem cell fraud later revealed about Piero Anversa's work.
