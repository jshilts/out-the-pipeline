This 2014 article discusses a developing case of scientific misconduct involving a 2011 *Science* paper on "reverse click" reactions using mechanical force to pull triazoles apart. ## 2. HISTORY

The Bielawski case subsequently unfolded as follows: In 2015, UT-Austin completed its investigation and the paper was formally retracted by *Science*. This case became part of broader discussions about mentor responsibility in graduate education.

More significantly, the subsequent decade saw an explosion of scientific misconduct revelations, many fitting the article's framework perfectly. Major cases included:
- The STAP stem cell scandal (Haruko Obokata, 2014), a clear Stage IIIb case with fabricated breakthrough results
- The reproducibility crisis in psychology (2015-2018), revealing widespread questionable research practices bordering on Stage I/II
- The Carmen Reinhart and Kenneth Rogoff Excel error controversy (2013), showing how even honest errors can have Stage II characteristics when results seem "too good to check"
- The Surgisphere COVID-19 data retractions (2020), a spectacular Stage IIIb fraud exploiting pandemic urgency
- The extensive work of Elisabeth Bik and others in systematically detecting image manipulation, revealing Stage I practices at industrial scale

The 2010s also saw the rise of "paper mills" and systematic fraud operations, particularly from certain institutions, that represented institutionalized versions of the article's Stage IIIa.
