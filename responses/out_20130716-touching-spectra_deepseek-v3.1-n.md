# Touching Up the Spectra (July 2013)

## 1. SUMMARY
This 2013 commentary addresses the long-standing problem of researchers manipulating analytical data—especially NMR (nuclear magnetic resonance) spectra—to hide impurities or unwanted peaks. The article highlights an editorial by Amos Smith, editor of *Organic Letters*, announcing that the journal had begun systematically checking supporting information and discovered multiple instances where spectra were digitally edited to remove evidence of impurities.

The editorial established that such data manipulation was unacceptable regardless of whether it affected experimental yields or conclusions, and that correspondent authors bear ultimate responsibility—rejecting excuses that students acted without supervision. The piece connects this behavior to toxic lab cultures where only "perfect" results are accepted, creating pressure to commit fraud. The author endorses Smith's position and suggests stronger penalties, including public corrections showing manipulated spectra and notifications to funding agencies and institutions.
